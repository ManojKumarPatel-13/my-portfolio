// About Me

Behind the Code

The intersection of logic and literature. A look into my philosophy, tech stack, and the stories that shape how I build. (Tap cards to explore).

My momentum stems from a stubborn fascination with how things function and form. During my eighth-grade lockdown, days dissolved into a dedicated loop of logic and literature. While I wrestled with the early syntax of HTML, CSS, and Python, an incredible mentor—Shraddha mam—gifted me my first programming book. That single push pivoted a passing curiosity into a lifelong obsession, with a copy of Harry Potter always resting beside my glowing screen.

Today, my ambition is absolute: I don't just want to write syntax; I want to engineer solutions. I spent my late nights relentlessly mastering the modern web stack, weaving together React, Next.js, Node, and Express. I thrive under pressure and actively hunt for hard technical challenges. Currently, my focus is architecting advanced backend systems and unraveling complex Data Structures and Algorithms in C++. I build logic that scales smoothly and survives stress.

When I step away from the terminal, I don't stop parsing information; I just switch mediums. Over the years, my bookshelf has evolved from childhood fantasies to expansive sci-fi universes and deeply emotional narratives. Yet, whether I am untangling a chaotic bug in a massive codebase or getting completely lost in the pages of a gripping novel, the underlying thrill of discovery remains exactly the same. I am an engineer fueled by the quiet rush of compiling clean code and concluding great chapters.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

> What's your timezone?

IST (UTC+5:30) — usually online through the evening, and happy to stretch for the right conversation across timezones.

> Are you open to freelance, collabs, or full-time work?

Yes — always up for interesting projects and conversations, But not ready for full-time yet.

> What's your current stack?

HTML, CSS, JavaScript & Express/MongoDB for full-stack web development. Python for scripting and automation, currently expanding into C++ and DSA.

> What are you currently reading?

Currently re-reading The Alchemist for the fifth time. It’s a great reminder of what it takes to pursue a vision, stay disciplined, and build things with purpose.

> Vim or VS Code?

VS Code, unapologetically. Ask me again in a year.

> Do your side projects always turn into obsessions?

Pretty much every time. Ask me about the "just a weekend" project that's now three months deep.

> What is your process for learning new technologies?

I tackle new technologies in three distinct phases: first, a high-level run to understand the syntax and ecosystem; second, a deep dive into core architectural concepts; and finally, a focus on implementation and polishing the codebase.
